About Bitumen Membranes

We offer bitumen membranes and other waterproofing solutions, catering to various industry needs.

Our Products

Waterproofing Membranes

We provide high-quality waterproofing membranes designed to protect against water damage in applications like basement walls, swimming pools, and water tanks.

Types :

Bituminous Waterproofing Membranes

Made from bitumen, these are ideal for below-ground applications like basements, tunnels, and parking garages.

Synthetic Waterproofing Membranes

Flexible membranes made from materials like PVC and TPO, used for roofs, balconies, and terraces.

Liquid Waterproofing Membranes

These membranes are applied as a liquid and cure into a seamless protective layer. Ideal for irregular surfaces and complex areas like roofs and balconies.

Modified Bitumen Membranes (MBMs)

These are enhanced asphalt materials, used in low-slope roofing and waterproofing, with excellent resistance to extreme temperatures and UV exposure.

MBMs are typically used in low-slope or flat roofing applications, although they can also be used in waterproofing applications such as basements, foundations, and tunnels. They are often installed as multi-layer systems that include a base layer, one or more reinforcing layers, and a surface layer.

One of the primary advantages of MBMs is their excellent resistance to temperature extremes and UV radiation, which makes them well-suited for use in areas with high sun exposure. They also have good flexibility and can resist cracking and splitting caused by freeze-thaw cycles.

There are several different types of MBMs available, including SBS (styrene-butadiene-styrene) and APP (atactic polypropylene) modified bitumen membranes.

SBS-modified Bitumen Membranes
  • More flexible than APP-modified bitumen membranes
  • Better suited for cold weather conditions
  • Made with synthetic rubber modifiers (styrene-butadiene-styrene)
APP-modified Bitumen Membranes
  • More heat-resistant than SBS-modified bitumen membranes
  • Better suited for warm weather conditions
  • Made with plastic modifiers (atactic polypropylene)
